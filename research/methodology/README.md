# Research Methodology

## Overview

Research approach for studying RSP Framework effectiveness and AI-Native development practices.

## Research Questions

### Primary Questions
1. What distinguishes AI-Native from AI-Assisted development?
2. What practices enable effective AI collaboration?
3. How can we systematically measure AI development effectiveness?

### Secondary Questions
- What patterns emerge in successful AI usage?
- How do teams adopt AI-Native practices?
- What are the barriers to adoption?
- What metrics indicate success?

## Research Approach

### 1. Case Study Method
Detailed examination of real-world adoption

**Data Collection**:
- Interviews with practitioners
- Observation of workflows
- Analysis of artifacts (prompts, code, etc.)
- Quantitative metrics

### 2. User Studies
Controlled studies of RSP Framework usage

**Methodology**:
- Pre/post surveys
- Task completion analysis
- Quality assessment
- Time tracking

### 3. Pattern Mining
Extracting successful patterns from usage

**Approach**:
- Analyze versioned prompts
- Identify effective structures
- Categorize patterns
- Validate with practitioners

### 4. Quantitative Analysis
Measuring impact and effectiveness

**Metrics**:
- Development velocity
- Code quality
- Bug rates
- Time savings
- Adoption rates

## Data Collection

### What We Collect
- Anonymous usage patterns
- Effectiveness metrics
- Survey responses
- Case study data

### What We Don't Collect
- Proprietary code
- Personal information
- Company secrets
- Sensitive data

### Privacy
- All data anonymized
- Aggregated analysis
- Ethical approval obtained
- Participant consent required

## Validation

### Triangulation
Multiple data sources and methods

### Peer Review
Expert validation of findings

### Practitioner Feedback
Real-world validation

## Ethics

- IRB approval for human subjects research
- Informed consent
- Data privacy protection
- No harm principle
- Transparency in reporting
